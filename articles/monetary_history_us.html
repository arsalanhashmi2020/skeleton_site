Monetary History of the United States

The *Monetary History of the United States*, written by Milton Friedman and Anna Schwartz, explores the role of money and monetary policy in U.S. economic history. It investigates the impact of monetary events on business cycles, inflation, and economic growth.

Summary

The *Monetary History of the United States* emphasizes the critical influence of the U.S. money supply on the nation’s economic fluctuations. It provides a detailed account of the Federal Reserve's actions and the effects of various monetary policies, particularly during the Great Depression, when monetary policy played a key role in exacerbating economic downturns. The book argues that the behavior of the money supply is often the driving factor behind economic booms and busts.

Key Themes

  • Monetary Policy and Economic Cycles: The book highlights how changes in the money supply can lead to periods of inflation, deflation, and economic instability.
  • Federal Reserve’s Role: A critical analysis of the Federal Reserve’s policies, particularly during major economic crises such as the Great Depression, is central to the work.
  • Money Supply and Business Cycles: The book explores how fluctuations in the money supply can lead to business cycles, including inflationary and deflationary periods.
  • Critique of Keynesian Economics: Friedman and Schwartz provide a critique of Keynesian economic models, particularly regarding their treatment of money supply and its relationship to the economy.

Impact on Economic Thought

The *Monetary History of the United States* significantly shaped the development of monetarist economic theory, advocating for the control of money supply to stabilize the economy. The work challenged the Keynesian emphasis on government intervention and fiscal policy, emphasizing instead the importance of stable monetary growth.

Notable Quotations

"The real distorting influence of monetary policy is not so much the money supply as the manner in which it is controlled." – Milton Friedman and Anna Schwartz
